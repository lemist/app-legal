NoteWhen Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 9, 2026

Summary: NoteWhen is a personal health event tracker. Your tracking data is stored on your devices and in your private iCloud account. With your permission, the app can read and write selected exercise, sleep, and water data with Apple Health. If you use AI monthly reports, an aggregated summary of your data is sent to our report-generation services to produce the report. We do not sell your data or use third-party analytics.

This Privacy Policy describes how NoteWhen ("we", "us", or "the app") handles information when you use the NoteWhen iOS app and related widget. By using the app, you agree to this policy.

1. Who we are

NoteWhen is an iOS app. For privacy questions, contact us via GitHub Issues.

2. Information you provide and we store

Depending on how you use the app, we process the following categories of data:

  • Health and wellness events you choose to track, such as symptoms, medications, blood pressure, bowel movements, menstrual periods, mood, anxiety questionnaires (PHQ-9 and GAD-7), sleep, exercise, water intake, meals, and notes you attach to each occurrence.
  • Event configuration, including event names, icons, categories, reminder schedules, and medication reminder times.
  • AI monthly reports, including generated report text and suggestions, if you use that feature.
  • App preferences, such as language, appearance, and reminder settings.

This data is stored locally on your device and, when you are signed in to iCloud, synced through your private iCloud account across your devices. We do not operate a separate account system or backend database for your tracking data.

3. Apple Health (HealthKit)

If you enable Health integration, NoteWhen may read workout, sleep, and water intake data from Apple Health to import into your event history. With your permission, the app may also write exercise, sleep, and water entries that you log in NoteWhen to Apple Health so your records stay in sync across apps.

  • Read access: imports workouts, sleep sessions, and water samples from Apple Health into NoteWhen.
  • Write access: saves workouts, sleep sessions, and water intake that you record in NoteWhen to Apple Health. Entries imported from Apple Health are not written back.
  • HealthKit data is processed on your device. Data you import or log is stored in your app database and iCloud as described above.
  • We do not use HealthKit data for advertising, and we do not sell HealthKit data.
  • You can grant, limit, or revoke read and write access for each data type at any time in the Health app or iOS Settings.

4. AI monthly reports

When you generate a monthly report, the app builds a text summary from your tracked events for the selected month and sends it over HTTPS to our report-generation services. Those services use third-party AI technology to produce the report. The request includes your selected app language and generation instructions; it does not include your Apple ID or iCloud credentials.

  • Report generation is designed to process requests transiently and not persist your tracking summary after the report is returned.
  • Generated reports are saved in your app data and synced via your iCloud account.
  • AI output is for personal reference only and is not medical advice.

You can avoid sending data for report generation by not generating monthly reports or by not subscribing to / using that feature.

5. Widget data

The NoteWhen widget displays quick-record shortcuts using a limited copy of event metadata (such as event name, icon, and sort order) shared between the app and the widget. Occurrence history is not stored for the widget.

6. Notifications

NoteWhen schedules local notifications on your device for event logging reminders, medication reminders, and optional monthly report availability. Notification content may include event or medication names you configured. We do not send push notifications from our own servers with your health data.

7. Subscriptions and payments

Optional subscriptions for AI monthly reports are processed entirely by Apple through your Apple ID. We receive subscription status from Apple’s StoreKit to unlock features. We do not receive or store your payment card details.

8. What we do not collect

  • We do not sell your personal or health data.
  • We do not use third-party advertising or analytics SDKs in the app.
  • We do not require you to create a separate NoteWhen account.

9. Data retention and deletion

  • Your tracking data remains until you delete it in the app, delete the app, or remove data from iCloud.
  • Events you delete are kept in an inactive state for 30 days before permanent deletion, as described in the app.
  • Deleting the app from a device removes local data on that device; iCloud copies may remain until you manage iCloud storage in iOS Settings.

10. Children

NoteWhen is not directed at children under 13 (or the minimum age required in your country). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

11. International users

Your iCloud data is handled under Apple’s privacy terms and the region associated with your Apple ID. AI report requests may be routed to servers outside your country when processed by our report-generation services.

12. Your choices

  • Choose which events to track and what details to record.
  • Disable iCloud sync by signing out of iCloud or disabling iCloud for NoteWhen in iOS Settings.
  • Revoke or limit HealthKit read and write access in the Health app or iOS Settings.
  • Turn off reminders in the app Settings.
  • Manage or cancel subscriptions in Settings > Apple ID > Subscriptions.

13. Medical disclaimer

NoteWhen is for personal tracking only. It is not a medical device and does not provide diagnosis or treatment. Always consult a qualified healthcare professional for medical decisions.

14. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The effective date at the top of this page will change when we do. Continued use of the app after an update means you accept the revised policy.
